When disaster strikes, dial ext. 17251 or run to 4D5

 

  • All equipment is plugged into a surge protector, which itself is plugged into the wall. Switch on at the wall when testing and switch off at the wall when finished for the day.

 

  • The “display” PC has 2 ethernet cards, one for eyelink (blue cable) and one to connect to the internet (yellow cable to the wall). The latter is disabled and/or the cable is unplugged.

The only reason for connecting to the internet is to download software updates! If and when this is necessary, make sure you: disable the LAN and/or unplug the cable once download is complete.

 

This machine is not intended for web browsing or e-mailing!

  • Note that this PC is not linked to the University network, so you cannot connect to the central back-up servers or any other machines on the network.

 

  • CRT monitors and Eyelink electronics need a warm-up period. Make sure the equipment is switched on at least 15 mins before testing.

 

  • Observe basic hygene:
    • We use masking tape to cover the chin and forehead rest of the Eyelink system - use new pieces for each participant.
    • Check whether the hot-plated mirror is free from dust, grease or anything else that should not be there.
    • Check whether the CRT screen is clean (you can use the same spray and cloth that is used to clean the mirror).

 

  • When testing, make sure the operator pc monitor is turned away from the experimental participant and the overall luminance level is set low (basically, we do not want any visible flicker or glow from the periphery for the observer).

 

  • Book your lab time through Google Calendar. Someone affiliated to the lab should have given you access. The convention is to put your name down (e.g. ‘CL’) for a number of slots you want to make available for your participants. Once you have received confirmation that testing will indeed happen at a particular time, you amend that particular time-slot to indicate that it is actually taken (e.g. ‘CL-confirmed’). The logic is that you need to reserve slots so you can offer multiple options to your participants, but you are unlikely to fill all of them, in which case other lab members can use the lab instead.

 

  • Make sure to back-up your data! Copy your data to some external device (e.g. a USB stick), transfer it to your desktop machine and then back up on a regular basis to your network drive.
